## 🏆 Top Recommendation: Automated Adversarial Red-Teaming for Generative Proposals

Deploy specialized AI agents designed to find flaws, hallucinations, and edge cases in every generative proposal before it reaches human eyes. This infrastructure ensures that the collapse of artifact costs is met with an equally low-cost, high-speed verification layer.

> Option 3 is the most strategic choice because it directly addresses the core problem: the 'collapse of artifact costs.' When the cost of generating proposals, code, and content drops to near zero, the volume of noise overwhelms human discernment. Option 3 provides a 'verification layer' that scales at the same speed and cost as the 'creation layer.' Unlike human-centric models (Options 2, 4, 10) which are difficult to scale, or purely economic models (Options 1, 7) which are prone to systemic gaming, adversarial red-teaming uses AI to audit AI. This creates a high-speed filter that ensures only high-quality, vetted proposals reach human decision-makers, effectively solving the discernment bottleneck while maintaining high organizational velocity.

## Summary
The transition from authority-gated to constraint-governed models is necessitated by the hyper-abundance of AI-generated artifacts. The session findings indicate that the primary organizational challenge has shifted from 'production' to 'discernment.' General trends across the options suggest a move toward algorithmic guardrails, reputation-based filtering, and the re-introduction of intentional friction (Proof-of-Humanity) for high-stakes decisions. The most effective strategies are those that leverage AI to manage the noise created by AI, ensuring that human attention is reserved for high-value synthesis and strategic accountability rather than routine vetting.
