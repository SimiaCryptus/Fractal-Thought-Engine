# Ohio Is Hell on Earth
*A secular single father navigates the "livestock logic" and moral-reform machinery of Westerville, Ohio, where institutional metrics and religious sorting transform a family into a "case."*
## Characters
- **Andrew**: Analytical, protective, deeply cynical of institutions. The narrator and father fighting for legibility. (Mid-40s, sharp features, wearing glasses and a slightly rumpled button-down shirt. A weary intellectual with tired but observant eyes.)
- **The Daughter**: Scientifically literate, oblivious to the bureaucracy, but feeling the social "remainder" status. The "output" the state is measuring. (7 years old, bright and curious. Wears a t-shirt with a T-Rex on it. Often carries a fossil or a science book.)
- **The Bureaucrat**: Cold, metric-driven, morally self-righteous. Representative of the State’s "livestock logic." (Features obscured by the glare of office lights. Wears a 1920s-style suit (Anti-Saloon League era) with a modern clinical white lab coat over it. Carries a clipboard that looks vaguely like a scythe.)
## Script
### Page 1
**Row 1**
- Panel 1: Extreme close-up of a growth chart. The lines look like a stock market crash. A red dot sits outside the "acceptable" curve.
- Panel 2: A clinical white hand holds a pen, circling the outlier dot with aggressive precision.
**Row 2**
- Panel 1: Andrew sits in a small plastic chair, looking up. The Doctor/Bureaucrat is a towering silhouette against a blinding fluorescent light.
- Panel 2: Andrew’s daughter sits on the exam table, swinging her legs. She’s wearing her T-Rex shirt. She looks perfectly healthy, but the Doctor is looking only at the chart.
  - *Caption*: I watched a doctor frown at a growth chart—my daughter’s growth chart—and I realized the concern had nothing to do with her health.
**Row 3**
- Panel 1: Surreal shift. The office walls peel away like old wallpaper to reveal a vast, muddy cattle feedlot under a grey Ohio sky.
- Panel 2: Close-up of the Daughter’s ear. A small, yellow plastic livestock tag is clipped to it. It reads: PERCENTILE: 15th. STATUS: INTERVENTION.
  - *Caption*: That is livestock logic. A farmer evaluating whether a calf is gaining weight on schedule. The child is not the patient. The child is the output.
### Page 2
**Row 1**
- Panel 1: A map of Westerville, Ohio. It’s being flattened by a giant, transparent grid descending from the sky.
- Panel 2: Andrew’s face, half-covered by the grid lines. His features are being converted into a barcode.
  - *Caption*: The first move of every modern state: make the population legible. Flatten the complexity of life into categories the institution can read.
**Row 2**
- Panel 1: A "Protected" family. A suburban house wrapped in a glowing web of golden threads. The threads connect to a church steeple and a school board office.
- Panel 2: An "Accommodated" family. An immigrant household. They are inside a box labeled "CULTURAL DIFFERENCE (ESL)." The Bureaucrat is checking a box on a form, nodding.
**Row 3**
- Panel 1: Andrew and his daughter standing in an open, empty field. No web, no box. Just a giant, harsh spotlight hitting them from above.
- Panel 2: The Bureaucrat stands at the edge of the light, holding a clipboard. He looks at Andrew with a squint of "deficiency."
  - *Caption*: Then there is the secular local family. Not protected by a church, not legible as "other." Our difference is read as a defect.
### Page 3
**Row 1**
- Panel 1: Andrew is in a glass observation room. He is packing a healthy lunch, helping with homework, and reading a bedtime story.
- Panel 2: Outside the glass, a group of Bureaucrats are looking at him. They don't see the lunch or the books. They only see a large, dark silhouette of a woman with a question mark over her face.
  - *Caption*: A single father is not a category. He is a question. The system cannot see paternal competence. It only sees the empty space where a mother should be.
**Row 2**
- Panel 1: A montage of forms. "Mother/Guardian" is highlighted in neon; "Father/Guardian" is a faint afterthought.
- Panel 2: A Bureaucrat leans in close to Andrew, his breath visible.
  - **The Bureaucrat**: "And where... is the mother, Mr. Charneski? Why are you the one here?"
**Row 3**
- Panel 1: A manila folder slams shut. The label reads: CHARNESKI, A. – SITUATION TO BE MANAGED.
  - *Caption*: The data said "competent father." The architecture said "situation." The architecture won every time.
### Page 4
**Row 1**
- Panel 1: A bright yellow school bus pulls up. It has "LIFEWISE ACADEMY" written on the side in a font that looks like a hymn book.
- Panel 2: Most of the children in the classroom stand up, glowing with a soft, communal light. They board the bus together.
**Row 2**
- Panel 1: Andrew’s daughter sits alone in a cavernous, dark classroom. The desks around her are empty. The clock on the wall is ticking loudly.
- Panel 2: She looks down at her T-Rex shirt. The dinosaur looks lonely.
  - *Caption*: The LifeWise kids return as a group. My daughter is not part of a group. She is a remainder. She is what is left when the community leaves the room.
**Row 3**
- Panel 1: A group of "Saved" children stand in a circle, whispering and pointing at the Daughter.
  - **Child 1**: "She believes in the old earth."
  - **Child 2**: "My mom says her house is... dangerous."
- Panel 2: Andrew’s daughter holds a small trilobite fossil, her knuckles white.
  - *Caption*: Her scientific curiosity is treated as evidence of abuse. My fitness is evaluated through the lens of a community I do not belong to.
### Page 5
**Row 1**
- Panel 1: The Anti-Saloon League headquarters in Westerville. A grim, stone building that looks like a fortress.
- Panel 2: A ghost-like figure in a 1920s top hat and coat stands in the window, looking out over the modern suburb.
  - *Caption*: Westerville calls itself the "Dry Capital of the World." It’s a boast. The civic DNA here is moral reform. Surveillance in the name of protection.
**Row 2**
- Panel 1: A modern kitchen. A pressure cooker sits on the stove, whistling violently.
- Panel 2: The Bureaucrat is taking notes on the steam.
  - *Caption*: The new temperance uses the school schedule to remove the secular from the social body. Building a pressure cooker and pathologizing the steam.
**Row 3**
- Panel 1: A neighbor peeks through closed blinds, holding a phone. Their face is twisted in "righteous concern."
- Panel 2: A CPS worker’s car idles at the curb of Andrew’s house. The headlights look like predatory eyes.
  - *Caption*: The enforcers are not aberrations. They are the immune system of the enclosure.
### Page 6
**Row 1**
- Panel 1: Andrew is walking down a staircase that descends into a dark pit. The walls are made of filing cabinets.
- Panel 2: Whispers emerge from the cabinets: "Unstable... Secular... Situation... Outlier..."
  - *Caption*: Layer one: Ambient toxicity. Layer two: Targeted misinterpretation. Layer three: Ideological offense.
**Row 2**
- Panel 1: Andrew and his daughter stand in their backyard. The wooden fence has transformed into barbed wire. A watchtower looms where the neighbor’s oak tree used to be.
- Panel 2: Andrew looks at the reader. His glasses are cracked. He looks exhausted but defiant.
  - **Andrew**: "Are we planning on eating the kids? No. We’re just processing them."
**Row 3**
- Panel 1: Close-up of the Daughter’s hand. She is holding a fossilized dinosaur tooth. It is sharp and real.
- Panel 2: Wide shot. Andrew and the Daughter stand together as the "machine"—a giant, mechanical Bureaucrat made of clipboards and gears—looms over them.
  - *Caption*: My daughter believes in dinosaurs. The system believes this is a problem. One of us is right. This is Ohio. The heart of it all. That is exactly where it hurts.
