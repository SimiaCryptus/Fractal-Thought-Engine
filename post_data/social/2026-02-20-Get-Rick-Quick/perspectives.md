When the gap between the working class and the "affluent" becomes a chasm that cannot be crossed by wages, the "leap" (GRQ) becomes the only rational—albeit statistically doomed—choice.
*   **The "Lottery-fication" of Class Mobility:** We are witnessing a shift where class transition is no longer viewed as a sociological process but as a stochastic event (a "win"). This devalues the ethical dignity of labor. If wealth is decoupled from productivity and tied to "alpha" or "luck," the moral justification for the existing class hierarchy evaporates, leaving only the raw power of capital.
*   **Predatory Inclusion:** Ethically, GRQ schemes (and their legalized counterparts like state lotteries or high-risk crypto-trading) practice "predatory inclusion." They offer the marginalized a seat at the table of capitalism, but only on terms that ensure their eventual liquidation. It is an ethical failure of a system to provide "hope" as a commodity to those it has structurally excluded from stability.

### 2. Risks: The Fragmentation of the Social Fabric

*   **The Erosion of Collective Agency:** The most significant sociological risk is the **atomization of the working class.** GRQ logic transmutes collective grievances (low wages, lack of healthcare) into individual "hustle" failures. The ethical risk here is a permanent rise in "deaths of despair" and mental health crises as individuals internalize the mathematical impossibility of the GRQ promise as a personal moral failing.
*   **The "Colosseum Effect" and Social Decadence:** Relying on the spectacle of the "big win" to maintain social order is a sign of a decadent society. When a civilization can no longer offer material progress to the majority and instead offers "secular liturgies" of sudden wealth, it loses its moral legitimacy. This eventually leads to radicalization when the "mirage of mobility" finally fades.

### 3. Opportunities: Reclaiming the Narrative

*   **The "Failure" as a Catalyst for Consciousness:** The inevitable failure of GRQ schemes provides a sociological opening. As more individuals realize the "table is tilted," there is an opportunity to redirect that frustration away from the self and toward **class consciousness.** The disillusionment following a "hype cycle" (like the NFT or meme-stock crashes) can be a fertile ground for re-introducing the value of collective bargaining and structural reform.
*   **Redefining "Wealth" vs. "Riches":** There is an ethical opportunity to re-socialize the concept of "the good life." By decoupling "success" from the "instant fortune" and re-linking it to "security, leisure, and community," we can begin to repair the social contract.

*   **Insight: GRQ as "Stupification" Policy.** GRQ is not an accident; it is a functional component of modern neoliberalism that prevents class conflict. It acts as a "firewall" against revolution by keeping the precarious focused on the "miracle" rather than the "policy."
*   **Recommendation: Regulatory Reframing.** We must move beyond viewing GRQ as "fraud" and start viewing it as a **public health and social stability issue.** Regulations should target the "hustle culture" marketing that equates financial risk-taking with moral virtue.
*   **Recommendation: Strengthening the "Floor," not the "Ceiling."** To combat the allure of GRQ, the social contract must be rebuilt by raising the floor (Universal Basic Services, living wages) so that the "leap" of the GRQ scheme is no longer the only perceived escape from poverty.
*   **Ethical Mandate:** Institutions (educational and governmental) must stop validating the "lottery logic" of the market. State-run lotteries, in particular, should be critiqued as an unethical "tax on hope" that undermines the state's duty to provide genuine paths to mobility.

5.  **Systemic Literacy Education:** Financial literacy must be evolved into "Systemic Literacy." Citizens should be taught to recognize the "Colosseum Effect" and the "Social Opiate" functions of markets to immunize them against the rhetorical hooks of the "Unique Mechanism."

### Final Insight
The prevalence of "Get Rich Quick" logic is the **smoke, not the fire.** It is a lagging indicator that the traditional social contract is dead. Until a new, viable path to stability is constructed for the majority, the "mirage of the leap" will remain the most powerful—and dangerous—narrative in the global economy.
