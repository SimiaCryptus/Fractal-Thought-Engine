For children remaining behind, this can lead to feelings of isolation or being "othered." For those participating, it can foster a sense of religious elitism or tribalism.

Shifting mid-day from a secular, inquiry-based academic environment to a dogmatic, faith-based environment can create significant cognitive dissonance. If the "theological inversion" mentioned in the subject involves a shift toward more punitive or extremist doctrines, this dissonance is amplified.
*   **The Erosion of the "Neutral Zone":** Public schools have traditionally served as a "neutral zone" where children of all backgrounds interact. The institutional capture of school boards to facilitate programs like LifeWise effectively dissolves this neutrality. Psychologically, the school ceases to be a shared community space and becomes a contested ideological battleground, which increases childhood anxiety.
*   **Parental Proxy vs. Child Autonomy:** Ethically, these programs operate on parental consent, but from a child welfare perspective, the child’s burgeoning autonomy is often ignored. A child may feel coerced to attend to please parents or peers, leading to "performative faith" which can be psychologically taxing and lead to long-term resentment or religious trauma.
