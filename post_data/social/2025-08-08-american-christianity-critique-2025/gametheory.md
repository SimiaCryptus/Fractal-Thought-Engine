*   **Game Type**: This is a **non-cooperative, non-zero-sum game**. *   **Sequential vs. Simultaneous**: The game is primarily **sequential**.

    2.  **ICL** moves second by choosing to accommodate, performatively resist, or reform.
    3.  **DFP (Dissenting Families/Parents)** move last, reacting to the environment created by the first two players.
*   **Repeated Game**: This is a **repeated game** played over election cycles and school years. This allows for "reputation building" and "tit-for-tat" strategies, though the "Protective Exit" often functions as a permanent exit from the game.
*   **Information Asymmetry**: There is significant **asymmetric information**. MPM has superior data on recruitment metrics and political mobilization. DFP has private information regarding their "breaking point"—the specific threshold of "human cost" (child distress) that will trigger an exit. ICL often suffers from "information silos," underestimating the rate of DFP's quiet withdrawal.
*   **Asymmetries**: There is a massive **power asymmetry**. MPM and ICL control financial and physical infrastructure ($500M+ in funding), while DFP’s power is primarily "negative power"—the power to withdraw legitimacy and future generations.
